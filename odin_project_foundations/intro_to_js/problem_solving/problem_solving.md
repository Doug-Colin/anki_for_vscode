## According to V. Anton Spraul in "Think Like a Programmer", what is problem solving?

Problem solving is writing an original program, that performs a particular set of tasks, and meets all stated constraints.

## What is the first step to solving a problem?

Understanding _exactly_ what the problem is.

## What is the second step to solving a problem?

_Planning_ how you are going to solve the problem.

## What is the third step to solving a problem?

Writing out the logic for your problem-solving program, in _natural language_, instead of code; a.k.a. pseudocoding. 

 [#problem-solving]() [#programming]() [#psuedocode]() 

<!------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -->
## What is the fourth and final step to solving a problem?

Coding! Turn your pseudo code into actual code by _decomposing_ each problem into smaller and easier to solve sub-problems, until the problem is solved (aka divide and conquer). 

 [#problem-solving]() [#programming]() [#psuedocode]()

## How can you make sure you are taking the first step to solving a problem, 'Understanding _exactly_ what the problem is', effectively?

By writing the problem down on paper, in plain English, and drawing diagrams if necessary, until you can explain the problem to someone else in plain English.

 [#problem-solving]() [#programming]() [#debugging]() 

<!------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- -->
## How can you make sure you are taking the second step to solving a problem, '_planning_ how to solve the problem', effectively?

By _explicitly_ considering and listing the program's requirements: UI functionality and appearance, inputs and their source, outputs and their destination, and finally, the steps that transform those inputs into output via code (psuedocode).

## How can you make sure you are taking the fourth step to solving a problem, turning psuedo-code into actual code, effectively?

Through decomposition, or 'dividing and conquering'.  _Decompose_ each problem into smaller and easier to solve sub-problems, until the problem is solved (aka divide and conquer).
